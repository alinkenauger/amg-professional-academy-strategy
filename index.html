Credibility model

What makes the certification real

AMG should make status mean something. The strongest version is not attendance-only. It should combine education, testing, proof, ongoing standards, and a public active-status directory.

  • Exam gates: written test, diagnosis test, teaching-video review, and practical case review.
  • Proof gates: documented student outcomes, before/after evidence, and coach reflection notes.
  • Active status: annual renewal, continuing education, standards review, and code of conduct.
  • Placement protection: only Elite or Placement Eligible instructors enter AMG-recommended roles.
  • Facility QA: partner facilities maintain reporting, staffing standards, curriculum standards, and customer experience standards.
Product family

Business of Golf

Business of Golf can become AMG's commercial education umbrella. The professional academy uses it for instructor and facility success, while a separate player-facing lane can teach career and golf-business skills without counting as instructor certification.

  • Instructors: lead generation, in-person sales, pricing, packaging, social authority, and retention.
  • Facilities: launch, operations, marketing, coaching department, hiring readiness, and partner status.
  • Players: sponsorship, NIL, tournament budgeting, personal brand, content, recruiting, and golf-career decisions.
  • Rule: player-facing Business of Golf courses should not unlock AMG instructor status unless they meet the professional assessment requirements.
Business model

How the money can work

The cleanest ecosystem has one-time education, annual active status, recurring facility support, and placement economics. That keeps the ladder valuable without forcing every offer into a subscription.

One-time education Courses, cohorts, specialist badges, launch sprints, and business modules.
Annual active status Renewal fee tied to CE, standards review, badge validity, and public directory listing.
Recurring facility layer Partner facility license, coaching department support, consulting retainer, or revenue share.
